Biography

José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda (June 19, 1861 – December 30, 1896) was a Filipino nationalist, writer, and revolutionary. He is widely considered one of the greatest heroes of the Philippines and was the founder of the Filipino nationalist movement. Rizal's literary works, particularly his novels Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo, sparked the Filipino revolution against Spanish colonial rule.

José Rizal was born on June 19, 1861, in Calamba, Laguna, Philippines. He was the seventh of eleven children of Francisco Mercado Rizal and Teodora Alonso Realonda. From an early age, Rizal showed exceptional intellectual ability and a passion for learning. He pursued higher education in Manila, then continued his studies in Europe, where he completed his medical degree.
